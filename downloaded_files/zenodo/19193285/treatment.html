Holotype card mounted. Right wing mounted on a microslide in Canada balsam and terminalia placed in a microvial with glycerin, both pinned along the specimen.

Diagnosis. Male. Occiput at its widest point 0.33× that of rest of head in lateral view. Coxae yellow, except hind coxae darker; femora yellow with sub-basal brown ring; tibiae yellow, slightly brown medially. Abdomen shiny black, elongated. Apices of surstyli slightly inward-directed; both surstyli with lower and upper margins curved and with apex rounded in lateral view. Gonopods sclerotized, with rounded apex.

Description. MALE (holotype). Habitus, left lateral view (Fig. 14A). Body length 5.4 mm. Head. Occiput at its widest point 0.33× that of rest of head in lateral view (Fig. 14A). Thorax. Postpronotal lobe brown. Wing (Fig. 3K). Length 7.2 mm, width 1.2 mm. Legs (Fig. 14A). Coxae yellow, except hind coxae darker; femora yellow with sub-basal brown ring; tibiae yellow, slightly brown medially. Abdomen (Fig. 14A–E). Tergite 5 wider than other tergites in dorsal view; sternites 6 and 7 as in Figs 14B–C, respectively. Syntergosternite 8 covered with few tiny setae. Terminalia (Figs 14F–L). Apices of surstyli slightly inward-directed; both surstyli with lower and upper margins curved and apex rounded in lateral view. Gonopods sclerotized, apex rounded. Apex of phallic guide developed, acute, with subapical lateral and apical internal setae. Ejaculatory ducts longer than phallic guide. Ejaculatory apodeme mushroom-shaped.

FEMALE. Similar to male, except for body length 5 mm, wing length 6.3 mm, width 1.4 mm, without the sub-basal brown ring spots on fore and mid femora. Terminalia (Fig. 18I–J). Ovipositor length 1 mm, piercer inward curved.

Taxonomic notes. Colombia (Cundinamarca), Ecuador (Imbabura) (Fig. 20).

Variation. Some male specimens with fore femur with sub-basal brown spots; Ecuadorian specimen with hind femur darker basally.

Etymology. From Latin ‘ flavus ’ = yellow and ‘ pes, pedis ’ = legs, in reference to the yellow legs. The name is to be treated as an adjective.

Taxonomic notes. See under Dorylomorpha (Paramuna) nigripes sp. nov.
