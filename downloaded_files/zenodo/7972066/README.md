# **Beetle Team Tribolium Data Archive**

The **Beetle Team Tribolium Data Archive** is a collection of 10 data sets created and/or analyzed over a 20 year period by a multidisciplinary group of collaborators, the "Beetle Team" (Jim Cushing, R. F. Costantino, Brian Dennis, Robert A. Desharnais, Shandelle M. Henson, Aaron A. King, and Jeffrey Edmunds). The data are from population laboratory experiments using the flour beetle *Tribolium castaneum*. Their research program was focused on providing biological evidence of complex nonlinear population dynamics.

The data archive is contined in a single Excel workbook, with a separate worksheet for each data set. See the original publications mentioned below for details on the methods and experimental design.



---


**Data Set 1**

*Relevant paper(s):*

Dennis, B., Desharnais, R.A., Cushing, J.M., Costantino, R.F. 1995. Nonlinear demographic dynamics: mathematical models, statistical methods, and biological experiments. Ecological Monographs 65, 261–282. https://doi.org/10.2307/2937060

Desharnais, R. A., and R. F. Costantino. 1980. Genetic analysis of a population of Tribolium: VII. Stability: Response to genetic and demographic perturbations. Canadian Journal of Genetics and Cytology 22:577–589. https://doi.org/10.1139/g80-063

*Summary:*

Experiment using perturbations to test for demographic stability. There was a control (4 replicates) and three demographic perturbations (3 replicates each). The demographic perturbations were imposed at week 10.

*Worksheet structure:*

Each row contains a census for a single culture. The columns are formatted as follows:

Col A:  Treatment coded as 1 = Control, 2 = 100 adults added at week, 10, 3 = all adults removed at week 10, 4 = all immatures removed at week 10
Col B:  Replicate coded as 1 = A, 2 = B, 3 = C, 4 = D (controls only)
Col C:  Week at census. The two week 10 entries are pre- and post-perturbation.
Col D:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col E:  Number of P-stage beetles
Col F:  Number of A-stage beetles

There are 273 records in the data set, not including the header row.

**Data Set 2**

*Relevant paper(s):*

Costantino, R.F., Cushing, J.M., Dennis, B., Desharnais, R.A. 1995. Experimentally induced transitions in the dynamic behaviour of insect populations. Nature 375, 227. https://doi.org/10.1038/375227a0

Dennis, B., Desharnais, R.A., Cushing, J.M., Costantino, R.F. 1997. Transitions in population dynamics: equilibria to periodic cycles to aperiodic cycles. Journal of Animal Ecology 66, 704–729. https://doi.org/10.2307/5923

*Summary:*

Experiment designed to generate changes in dynamic behavior by changing a life history parameter, adult mortality (mua). Adult mortality was manipulated beginning at week 12.  There was a control
and five adult mortality treatments: mua = 0.04, 0.27, 0.50, 0.73, and 0.96.  Two genetic strains were used:  RR and SS.  Each combination of mortality treatment and genetic strain was replicated three times.

*Worksheet structure:*

Each row contains a census for a single culture. The columns are formatted as follows:

Col A:  Genetic strain coded as 1 = RR, 2 = SS
Col B:  Treatment coded as 1 = Control, 2-6 = adult mortality rates of 0.04, 0.27, 0.50, 0.73, 0.96
Col C:  Replicate number (1-24 for each strain)
Col D:  Week at census. The two week 12 entries are pre- and post-treatment.
Col E:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col F:  Number of P-stage beetles
Col G:  Number of A-stage beetles

There are 960 records in the data set, not including the header row.

**Data Set 3**

*Relevant paper(s):*

Costantino, R.F., Desharnais, R.A., Cushing, J.M., Dennis, B. 1997. Chaotic dynamics in an insect population. Science 275, 389–391. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.275.5298.389

Dennis, B., Desharnais, R.A., Cushing, J.M., Henson, S.M., Costantino, R.F. 2001. Estimating chaos and complex dynamics in an insect population. Ecological Monographs 71, 277–303. https://doi.org/10.1890/0012-9615(2001)071[0277:ECACDI]2.0.CO;2

*Summary:*

Experiment designed to generate changes in dynamic behavior by changing a life history parameter, adult rate of pupal cannibalism (cpa). In this experiment, adult mortality (mua) and adult recruitment (cpa) were manipulated in the experimental cultures.  There was a control with no manipulations and and seven treatments with cpa = 0.00, 0.05, 0.10, 0.25, 0.35, 0.50, 1.00 and mua = 0.96 for all values of cpa. The controls and each cpa treatment were replicated three times.

*Worksheet structure:*

Each row contains a census for a single culture. The columns are formatted as follows:

Col A:  Cpa treatment coded as 1 = Control, 2-8 = 0.00, 0.05, 0.10, 0.25, 0.35, 0.50, 1.00
Col B:  Replicate number (1-24 for each strain)
Col C:  Value of parameter mua (estimated for controls)
Col D:  Value of parameter cpa (estimated for controls)
Col E:  Week at census
Col F:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col G:  Number of P-stage beetles
Col H:  Number of A-stage beetles

There are 984 records in the data set, not including the header row.

**Data Set 4**

*Relevant paper(s):*

Desharnais, R.A., Costantino, R.F., Cushing, J.M., Henson, S.M., Dennis, B. 2001. Chaos and population control of insect outbreaks. Ecology Letters 4, 229–235. https://doi.org/10.1046/j.1461-0248.2001.00223.x

*Summary:*

Experiment designed to test the use of small perturbations to control chaotic population dynamics. In this experiment, two control methods were tested: (1) add 3 adults when L-stage numbers were 150 or less and A-stage numbers were 3 or less (in-box) and (2) add  adults if L-stage numbers exceeded 150 or A-stage numbers exceeded 3 (out-box). There was a control with no manipulations. The control methods were applied at week 134 and thereafter until week 210. Each treatment was replicated three times.

*Worksheet structure:*

Each row contains a census for a single culture. The columns are formatted as follows:

Col A:  Treatment coded as 1 = Control, 2 = in-box, 3 = out-box
Col B:  Replicate number
Col C:  Week at census
Col D:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col E:  Number of P-stage beetles
Col F:  Number of A-stage beetles

There are 954 records in the data set, not including the header row.

**Data Set 5**

*Relevant paper(s):*

Desharnais, R.A., Costantino, R.F., Cushing, J.M., Henson, S.M., Dennis, B., King, A.A. 2006. Experimental support of the scaling rule for demographic stochasticity. Ecology Letters 9, 537–547. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1461-0248.2006.00903.x

*Summary:*

Experiment designed to test the inverse square root rule for demographic stochasticity. In this experiment, two volumes of flour medium were used: (1) 20g and (2) 60g. Populations had chaotic dynamics. The populations were maintained for 108 weeks. Each treatment was replicated three times.

Col A:  Treatment coded as 1 = 20g medium, 2 = 60g medium
Col B:  Replicate number
Col C:  Week at census
Col D:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col E:  Number of P-stage beetles
Col F:  Number of A-stage beetles

There are 330 records in the data set, not including the header row.

**Data Set 7**

*Relevant paper(s):*

Costantino, R.F., Cushing, J.M. Dennis,B. Desharnais, R.A. and Henson, S.M. 1998. Resonant population cycles in temporally fluctuating habitats. Bulletin of Mathematical Biology 60, 247–273. https://doi.org/10.1006/bulm.1997.0017

Jillson, D.A. 1980. Insect populations respond to fluctuating environments. Nature 288, 699–700. https://doi.org/10.1038/288699a0

*Summary:*

These data were from a study of the effects of period-two cycling of habitat size on populations with intrinsic period-two cycling, with a focus on population resonance. The data are a subset of the results from Jillson (1980). There were two treatments, a constant habitat size of 20g of medium and a period-two habitat size that alternated between 32g and 8g of medium. Alternating habitat sizes were started at week 18. There were four replicates per treatment group. 

*Worksheet structure:*

Each row contains a census for a single culture. The columns are formatted as follows:

Col A:  Treatment coded as 1 = constant 20g medium, 2 = 32g-8g-... medium
Col B:  Replicate number
Col C:  Week at census
Col D:  Habitat size (grams of medium to which beetles are returned)
Col E:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col F:  Number of P-stage beetles
Col G:  Number of A-stage beetles

There are 288 records in the data set, not including the header row.

**Data Set 8:**

*Relevant paper(s):*

Henson, S.M., Costantino, R.F., Cushing, J.M., Dennis, B., and Desharnais, R.A. 1999. Multiple attractors, saddles, and population dynamics in periodic habitats. Bulletin of Mathematical Biology 61, 1121–1149. https://doi.org/10.1006/bulm.1999.0136

*Summary:*

These data were from a study of the effects of period-two cycling of habitat size on populations with intrinsic period-two cycling, with a focus on multiple attractors. The experimental design is given in Table 1 of Henson et al. (1999). There were two crossed factors: (1) habitat sequence (HS), with three levels, and (2) initial condition (IC), with two levels. There were three replicates per treatment group. All replicates were maintained for at least 76 weeks. One replicate from each treatment group was maintained for 140 weeks.

*Worksheet structure:*

Each row contains a census for a single culture. The columns are formatted as follows:

Col A:  HS coded as 1 = constant 20g , 2 = 28g-12g-... , and 3 = 32g-8g-...
Col B:  IC (Lo,Po,Ao) coded as 1 = (150,200,150) and 2 = (150,0,150)
Col C:  Replicate number
Col D:  Week at census
Col E:  Habitat size (grams of medium to which beetles are returned)
Col F:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col G:  Number of P-stage beetles
Col H:  Number of A-stage beetles

There are 894 records in the data set, not including the header row.

**Data Set 10:**

Alternating habitat sizes were started at week 18. There were four replicates for the constant habitat treatment group and six replicates for the period-four habitat treatment group. 

*Worksheet structure:*

Each row contains a census for a single culture. The columns are formatted as follows:

Col A:  Treatment coded as 1 = constant 20g medium, 2 = 32g-32g-8g-8g-... medium
Col B:  Replicate number
Col C:  Week at census
Col D:  Habitat size (grams of medium to which beetles are returned)
Col E:  Number of L-stage beetles
Col F:  Number of P-stage beetles
