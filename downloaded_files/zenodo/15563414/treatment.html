Diagnosis. This species is distinguished from all other species in the genus Luispenaia by the following combination of characters: body flattened, dorsally dark brown or black on head, pronotum and scutellum, legs, elytra light brown, shiny. Clypeus distally straight and upturned, with lateral margins rounded and reflexed upturned; lateral margin between clypeal base and ocular canthus very projected as external rounded lobule. Antennae with eight antennomeres; club with three antennomeres. Pronotum approximately subhexagonal, laterally angled, with sparse dorsal setae.

Full description and figures. Mondaca et al. 2019: 554, 556–557; figs. 11, 15, 21–23.
