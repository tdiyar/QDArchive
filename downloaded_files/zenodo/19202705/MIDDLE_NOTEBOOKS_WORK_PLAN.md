# WORK PLAN: GRANULAR MULTI-PASS RE-ENGINEERING OF NB II–VII

## Governing Principle

Each notebook gets 3–4 passes. Each pass is a DISTINCT TRANSFORM with its own algorithm. The passes are NOT cumulative applications of the same tool — they are different LENSES, each revealing and fixing a different species of regularity.

The passes, in order:

1. **ARCHIVE ERUPTION** — Insert archive material (the beautiful miscellany) that disrupts the analytical register by arriving from a completely different velocity, tone, and angle.

2. **ENACTMENT PASS** — Find every place the book DECLARES its own danger/power/operativity and either CUT the declaration or REPLACE it with a passage that ENACTS what it was declaring.

3. **PROSODIC GRANULAR** — Apply the four-term algorithm at paragraph level, one section at a time, using the section-by-section conceptual mode map.

4. **CROSS-NOTEBOOK DISRUPTION** — Insert material from OTHER notebooks into THIS notebook: a NB VIII wound arriving in NB III, a NB IX fragment erupting in NB V. Breaking the notebooks' internal hermetic seal.

## KEY INSIGHT: WHY FOUR PASSES INSTEAD OF ONE

One pass applies one lens. The uniformity has FOUR species:

1. **Tonal uniformity** — the analytical register never breaks. Archive eruptions fix this by introducing voices from completely different velocities (liturgical, comic, confessional, pre-theoretical).

2. **Declarative uniformity** — the book tells instead of showing. Enactment passes fix this by converting "this is dangerous" into passages that make the reader feel danger.

3. **Prosodic uniformity** — sentences at identical cadence, paragraphs at identical length. The four-term algorithm fixes this by applying Marx's prosodic DNA at the paragraph level.

4. **Hermetic uniformity** — each notebook seals itself. Cross-notebook disruptions fix this by letting material from other notebooks leak in — a wound from NB VIII arriving in NB III, a fragment from NB IX erupting in NB V.

Each species of uniformity is invisible to the lens designed for the others. A prosodic transform won't fix tonal uniformity. An archive eruption won't fix hermetic uniformity. The four passes are necessary because the uniformity is four-dimensional.
