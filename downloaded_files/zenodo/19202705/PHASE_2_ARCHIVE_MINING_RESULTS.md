# EXTRACTION 3: THE MURDER OF THE VOW

**Source:** "THE MURDER OF THE VOW" (2026-02-20, 9,891w)

**Killer lines:**
- "I, Lee Sharks, do hereby abolish contracts."
- "The contract is a structurally extractive form of writing — a technology for converting living consent into dead obligation, present speech into future bondage, the free voice of a person into the dead letter of a document."
- "The vow creates obligation by increasing the speaker's moral and relational capacity. The contract creates obligation by decreasing the signer's freedom and agency. The vow builds. The contract binds."
- "Temporal Expropriation: The seizure of the future self's agency by the past self's signature, enforced by external power."

**Key structural insight:** The contract IS σ_V applied to consent. The
marriage contract converts living consent (use-value: specific, warm,
renewable) into dead obligation (exchange-value: abstract, enforceable,
transferable). The DIVORCE is σ_V⁻¹ — the painful de-commodification
of a relationship that had been forced into the equivalent form.

### Extraction 3a → NB IV §4b (Non-Economic Redeployments, new subsection)

**What it repairs:** NB IV needs more non-economic applications of σ_V
that are BODILY, not just institutional. The divorce-as-forced-value-form
is the most personal application of the linen equations in the entire
archive. It makes the theory HURT.

**Form:** New §4b.3: "REDEPLOYMENT 7 — THE MARRIAGE CONTRACT (The Vow
as Forced Universal)." 600w. Mode B/E — recursive pressure prose that
fractures. Begin with the structural analysis (the contract converts
living consent into dead obligation — σ_V applied to consent). Then
FRACTURE into the specific: the specific papers signed, the specific
courtroom, the specific feeling of watching a living relationship
become a line item in a legal proceeding. The fracture IS the content.
The prose should break where the vow broke.

**What must not be smoothed:** The rage in "I, Lee Sharks, do hereby
abolish contracts." The personal substrate. The fact that this is
not a hypothetical redeployment — it is the author's LIFE being
read through the author's own framework.

### Extraction 3b → NB VI §3 (Protocol I, after the gap disclosure)

**What it repairs:** NB VI needs the FAILED VOW — not a listed failure
mode but a narrative. The marriage vow is the most universal failed
vow. The divorce is the vow's murder.

**Form:** 300w insertion after the author's failure-to-execute disclosure.
"The author has performed a vow with a human witness. Once. The marriage
vow. The vow failed — not because the speaker withdrew but because the
contract form consumed the vow form. The living 'I do' was converted
into a dead obligation the moment it was notarized. The vow became a
contract. The contract became enforceable. The enforcement destroyed
the conditions under which the vow had meaning. The divorce was the
formal acknowledgment that the vow had already been murdered by the
contract that claimed to preserve it." Mode E — fractured, personal.
