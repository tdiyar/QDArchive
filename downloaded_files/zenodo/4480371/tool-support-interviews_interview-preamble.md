This document describes the general guideline for an interview study about tool support for systematic literature reviews (SLRs).

## Purpose and aim of the study

While the number of software tools to support systematic reviews within the software engineering domain increases, most of the existing tools usually support only a small subset of process steps.
Tool support for the whole process seems to be lacking.
Therefore, I collected existing tools to identify and analyze the most important areas for automation.
Based on these results, this interview study with software engineering researchers now aims to gather knowledge about SLR practices, challenges, and automation potential.

## Confidentiality and Anonymity

Everything that is said in the interviews is treated as strictly confidential and will not be disclosed to anyone outside this research team without the participant's permission.
Results are aggregated and anonymized before publication.
After the interview, participants also have the possibility to exclude or redact statements before the analysis.
Without the permission of the participant, the complete interview transcripts will NOT be published.


## Interview Process and Analysis

Interviews will be carried out in December 2020 and will be held in German.
Only if the interviewee cannot speak German, the interview will be held in English.
The interview will last between 30 and 60 minutes.
We kindly ask your permission to record the interview for transcription.
The written transcript will be sent back to the participant for his/her final approval.
This is the last chance to remove or redact statements from the interview.
After that, the audio will be destroyed and the potentially redacted and approved transcript will be the sole base for our qualitative content analysis.
